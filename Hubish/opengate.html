Time Loops

Time loops are anomalies where a sequence of events repeats endlessly. Understanding the cause and the nature of a loop is crucial to resolve it.

  • Repetition of events within a fixed period.
  • Memory retention by some individuals within the loop.
  • Potential to alter the sequence with interventions.

Temporal Immunity

Temporal immunity occurs when individuals become impervious to physical harm or changes in their timeline. This can cause disruptions and paradoxes.

Resolution Protocol:

  1. Identify the source of immunity.
  2. Isolate the individual from other time anomalies.
  3. Use specialized equipment to neutralize immunity.

Temporal Distortions

Temporal distortions involve anomalies where individuals or environments behave unpredictably, often losing coherence with the timeline.

Key Indicators:

  • Erratic memory loss or gain.
  • Environmental inconsistencies.
  • Unusual shifts in time perception.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: What should I do if I encounter a new anomaly?
A: Report it immediately to your supervisor and document all observable effects.

Q: Can anomalies affect equipment?
A: Yes, ensure all devices are shielded from temporal interference.
